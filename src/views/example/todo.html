Production Bundling

Bundling your project for production and deployment is made easy with jspm. Running the follow from your terminal or console will clean your dist directory and bundle the project which would be equivalent to running $ jspm bundle-sfx app/app app.js directly using the jspm cli.

Once the below is completed you're ready to deploy your /dist directory to your procuction environment. 

        $ gulp prod

Sass & Styling

One of the best things to add to your workflow is to add Sass for your styling. Sass works particularly well for complex layouts. It makes your source more readable not to mention powerful.

Below is an example of what a gulp.task may look like for your styles. NOTE: this assumes you are using the gulp-load-plugins module. What this module does is look in your package.json and any module prefixed with gulp- it exposes to your gulpfile.js. If you do not wish to use this module simply require the Sass module which would be var sass = require ('gulp-sass') 

        gulp.task('styles', function () {

         gulp.src(['./path/to/src/*.sass])

          .pipe(plugins.sass())

          .pipe(gulp.dest('./path/to/dist');

        });
